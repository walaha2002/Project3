Findings and Analysis



Selecting the Best Model

The models were evaluated based on their accuracy, precision, and efficiency. As shown above, the multiple linear regression model had a much lower overall score than the other models. Based on the low score, it was eliminated from the next evaluation steps. The gradient boosting model performed well predicting students who would pass but did not reliably identify the students who would fail. With the multi-linear regression and gradient boosting models eliminated, the random forest and extremely randomized tree models were the final candidates. They have similar scores and predictive power. Ultimately, the extremely random trees model was selected since the modelling algorthim is more efficient and will return results faster.

Findings



The model identified cumulative GPA as the primary predicitve characteristic with entrance test score and the amount of time taken to complete the program as secondary characteristics. The two charts above show the feature importance and permutation importance of all of the charactistics considered. The higher the values that characteristic has more influence on the final prediction. While there are some minor differences between these measures of importance, the most influential characteristics are apparent.

Surprisingly, previous education of students did not influence the predictions of the model in a significant way. This could be due to the very high number of students entering the program with High School / some college educational attainment. This group is far more than half the students included in the original data. The low influence of educational characteristcs could also suggest that previous education is not a strong predictor of success on the certification exam.



Cumulative GPA and Likely Outcomes
Cumulative GPA Prediction
3.3 and above Likely to Pass
2.5 to 3.3 Uncertain

To better understand the influence of cumulative GPA, we compiled the predictions for an average student with a range of GPAS. The table above summarizes the predictions and probability of those predictions. The predictions for the range between 2.5 and 3.0 had probabilities between 50% and 60% which means the model is very close random chance when predicting those outcomes. Outside of that range, the model becomes much more confident.

Final Conclusions

This modelling effort revealed the importance of cumulative GPA in predicting students' performance on the certification exam. Students with a cumulative GPA at or below 2.5 are especially at risk of failing on their first attempt on the exam. Targetting interventions for this group such as academic counselling and student support resources may help improve GPAs which should improve exam outcomes.

This work could be expanded in a couple ways. Examining trends in GPA as students progress through the program may uncover points where interventions could improve outcomes. Secondly, data on clinicals was not included in this analysis. Since they provide a unique experience to students, differences in clinicals should be explored for possible influence on certification outcomes.
